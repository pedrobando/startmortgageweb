# 7 First-Time Home Buyer Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

*By [Jexayra Rivera](/about), Branch Manager | NMLS# 1631454*

Buying your first home should be exciting. Not stressful.

But after 9 years of helping families through the mortgage process, I have seen the same mistakes pop up over and over. The good news is that every single one is easy to avoid — once you know what to watch for.

Here are the 7 most common first-time home buyer mistakes and how to steer clear of each one.

---

## Mistake 1: Not Getting Pre-Approved Before You Start Looking

This is the biggest one.

You find a home you love. You rush to make an offer. Then you discover you do not qualify — or you qualify for less than you expected. The home slips away.

**Pre-approval** tells you exactly how much you can borrow before you start shopping. It also proves to sellers that you are serious and able to close.

In a competitive market, an offer without a pre-approval letter often gets ignored.

**How to avoid it:** [Get pre-approved](/pre-approval) before you tour a single home. At START Mortgage, most pre-approvals are ready within 24 hours. Subject to credit approval.

---

## Mistake 2: Maxing Out Your Budget

A lender says you qualify for $400,000. So you go looking for homes at $400,000.

That is a mistake.

The lender does not know about your grocery bills, childcare costs, car insurance, or the family vacations you look forward to every year. Buying at the very top of your limit can leave you feeling squeezed every single month.

**How to avoid it:** Set your budget based on what you are comfortable paying each month — not the maximum number on your pre-approval letter. Leave room to breathe. Our post on [how much house you can afford](/blog/how-much-house-can-i-afford) walks you through the math step by step.

---

## Mistake 3: Ignoring Your Credit Score

Your [credit score](/credit-readiness) affects whether you qualify and what interest rate you get. A few points can mean thousands of dollars over the life of your loan.

Too many first-time buyers never check their credit until it is time to apply — and then discover surprises that could have been fixed months earlier.

**How to avoid it:** Check your credit early. If there are errors, dispute them. If your score needs work, take a few months to build it before you apply. We review your credit during your [planning session](/planning-session) and show you exactly where you stand.

---

## Mistake 4: Not Shopping Your Loan Options

Not all loans are the same. Different [loan programs](/loan-programs) have different rules for down payments, interest rates, and mortgage insurance.

For example:
- **[FHA loans](/loan-programs/fha)** let you put as little as 3.5% down
- **[Conventional loans](/loan-programs/conventional)** let you drop mortgage insurance once you hit 20% equity
- **[VA loans](/loan-programs/va)** offer zero down for eligible veterans

If you only look at one option, you might miss a better fit.

**How to avoid it:** Work with a mortgage broker who compares options from multiple lenders. At START Mortgage, we are a broker — which means we shop for you and find the program that fits your situation. Not just one option from one bank.

---

## Mistake 5: Making Big Purchases Before Closing

This catches people off guard every time.

You are pre-approved. You found a home. Closing is three weeks away. So you buy a new car. Or open a new credit card. Or finance a living room set.

Any of those moves can change your debt-to-income ratio and your credit score. That can put your mortgage approval at risk — even after you have been pre-approved.

**How to avoid it:** Do not make any major purchases, open new credit accounts, or change jobs between pre-approval and closing. Wait until you have the keys in your hand. Then celebrate.

---

## Mistake 6: Forgetting About Closing Costs

Your down payment is not the only cash you need at the table. **Closing costs typically run between 2% and 5% of the home price.**

On a $300,000 home, that is $6,000 to $15,000. These costs cover things like:

- Appraisal fees
- Title insurance
- Lender fees
- Prepaid taxes
- Homeowner's insurance

If you do not plan for these costs, closing day can hit you with a surprise bill. Our post on [what to expect at closing](/blog/what-to-expect-at-closing) breaks down every cost so you know what is coming.

**How to avoid it:** Ask your mortgage professional about estimated closing costs early in the process. We break down every dollar so there are no surprises.

---

## Mistake 7: Skipping the Home Inspection

A home might look perfect on the surface. But underneath, there could be a leaky roof, faulty wiring, or a cracked foundation.

A professional home inspection costs a few hundred dollars. Skipping it to "save money" can cost you thousands later.

**How to avoid it:** Always get a professional home inspection. If the inspector finds problems, you can negotiate repairs with the seller — or walk away if the issues are too serious.
