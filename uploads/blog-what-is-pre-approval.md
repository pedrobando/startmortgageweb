---

## What Is Mortgage Pre-Approval?

**Mortgage pre-approval** is when a lender reviews your real finances and tells you how much you can borrow. Not a guess. Not an estimate. A verified number based on your income, debts, credit score, and savings.

You get a letter that says: "This buyer is approved for up to this amount."

That letter does two things:

- **It gives you a clear budget** so you only look at homes you can actually afford.
- **It shows sellers you are serious** — and that your offer has real backing behind it.

Think of pre-approval as your green light. Without it, you are guessing. With it, you shop with confidence.

---

## Pre-Approval vs. Pre-Qualification: Know the Difference

These two terms sound alike. They are not the same.

| | Pre-Qualification | Pre-Approval |
|---|---|---|
| **What happens** | You share basic info about your finances | A lender verifies your documents, income, and credit |
| **How long it takes** | A few minutes | Usually 24 hours |
| **What you get** | A rough estimate | A verified letter with a specific amount |
| **How sellers see it** | Weak — it is just a guess | Strong — it is backed by real data |

**Bottom line:** Pre-qualification is an opinion. Pre-approval is proof. When you make an offer on a home, sellers want proof.

---

## Why Do Realtors Ask for Pre-Approval?

Most realtors will not show homes until you are pre-approved. Here is why:

- **It saves everyone time.** You only tour homes in your price range.
- **It makes your offer stronger.** Sellers pick buyers who can close.
- **It speeds up the process.** Once you find the right home, you can move fast.

At START Mortgage, we do not issue pre-approvals we cannot close. When a realtor sees our letter, they know it is solid. Your offer stands out from the crowd. Subject to credit approval.

## What You Need to Get Pre-Approved

The process is simpler than most people think. Here is what to gather:

### Documents You Will Need

- **Pay stubs** — last 30 days
- **W-2s or tax returns** — past two years
- **Bank statements** — last two months
- **Government-issued ID** — driver's license or passport
- **Proof of other income** — child support, rental income, etc. (if applicable)

### What the Lender Reviews

- Your **credit score** and history
- Your **monthly income** vs. your monthly debts (called your debt-to-income ratio)
- Your **savings** for a down payment and [closing costs](/blog/what-to-expect-at-closing)
- Your **job history**

That is it. No mystery. No tricks. Just numbers.

## Common Questions About Mortgage Pre-Approval

### Does pre-approval hurt my credit?

Pre-approval involves a hard credit check. It may lower your score by a few points. But if you are shopping across lenders in a short window (usually 14 to 45 days), multiple checks often count as one inquiry.

### How long does a pre-approval last?

Most pre-approval letters are valid for 60 to 90 days. If yours expires before you find a home, we can refresh it quickly.

### Does pre-approval mean I am approved for a loan?

No. Pre-approval means you are likely to be approved based on the info you provided. Final approval happens after you find a home and the lender completes full underwriting. This is not a commitment to lend. Subject to credit approval.

### Can I get pre-approved with a low credit score?

It depends on the loan program. [FHA loans](/loan-programs/fha) allow credit scores as low as 580 with 3.5% down. We review your full picture — not just one number — during your [planning session](/planning-session).
