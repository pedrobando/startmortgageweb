# What to Expect at Your Mortgage Closing (A Step-by-Step Guide)

*By [Jexayra Rivera](/about), Branch Manager | NMLS# 1631454*

You made it.

You found a home. You got approved. And now closing day is almost here.

If this is your first time, you might be wondering: What actually happens? What will I sign? How much will I pay? How long does it take?

Here is the full picture — step by step — so there are zero surprises when you sit down at that table.

---

## What Is a Mortgage Closing?

Closing is the final step in buying a home. It is the day you:

- Sign all the paperwork
- Pay your closing costs and down payment
- Officially become a homeowner

You might hear people call it "settlement" or "closing on the house." It all means the same thing: the deal is done, and the home is yours.

---

## What Happens Before Closing Day

A few things need to happen before you sit at the table.

### You Will Review the Closing Disclosure

At least **three business days** before closing, you will receive a document called the **Closing Disclosure (CD)**. This is a detailed breakdown of:

- Your final loan terms
- Your monthly payment
- Every cost you will pay at closing

**Read it carefully.** Compare it to the Loan Estimate you received when you first applied. If anything looks different or confusing, call your mortgage professional right away.

At START Mortgage, we walk you through the Closing Disclosure before closing day. You will understand every number before you pick up the pen.

### You Will Do a Final Walk-Through

Most buyers do a final walk-through of the home **24 to 48 hours** before closing. This is your chance to confirm:

- Agreed-upon repairs have been made
- The home is in the same condition as when you made your offer
- Nothing is missing or damaged

This walk-through is quick — usually 30 minutes or less. But it matters.

---

## What Happens on Closing Day

Here is what to expect when you arrive.

### Who Will Be There

Depending on your state and situation, the people at the table may include:

- **You** (the buyer)
- **The seller** (or their representative)
- **A closing agent or attorney** (handles the paperwork)
- **Your realtor**
- **The seller's realtor**

Your mortgage professional may also be there in person or available by phone. At START Mortgage, we make sure you have support on closing day.

### How Long Does It Take?

Most closings take **30 to 90 minutes**. It depends on the number of documents and whether any last-minute questions come up. Plan to block out about two hours so you do not feel rushed.

---

## Documents You Will Sign

You will sign a stack of papers. Here are the most important ones:

### The Promissory Note

Your promise to repay the loan. It includes your:
- Interest rate
- Monthly payment amount
- Loan term (usually 15 or 30 years)
- What happens if you miss payments

### The Deed of Trust (or Mortgage)

This gives the lender a claim on the property until you pay off the loan. It is what secures the loan to the home.

### The Closing Disclosure

You reviewed this before closing. Now you sign it to confirm the final numbers.

### Title Documents

These transfer ownership from the seller to you. The title company makes sure there are no liens or claims against the property.

### Other Standard Forms

Forms related to property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and any HOA agreements.

**Take your time.** Read what you are signing. If something does not look right, ask before you sign. That is what we are here for.

---

## Closing Costs: What Will You Pay?

Closing costs typically run **2% to 5%** of the home's purchase price. On a $300,000 home, that is **$6,000 to $15,000**.

Here is what those costs cover:

| Cost | What It Pays For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Lender fees** | Processing and underwriting your loan |
| **Appraisal fee** | Confirms the home's market value |
| **Title insurance** | Protects you and the lender from title disputes |
| **Title search** | Checks for liens or claims on the property |
| **Escrow deposits** | Prepaid property taxes and homeowner's insurance |
| **Recording fees** | Government charges to record the deed |
| **Prepaid interest** | Interest from closing day to the end of the month |
| **Homeowner's insurance** | Your first year's premium (usually paid upfront) |

Every one of these costs is listed on your Closing Disclosure. You will know the exact amounts before closing day. No surprises.

Subject to credit approval. Programs, rates, and terms subject to change.

---

## What to Bring to Closing

Here is your checklist:

- **Government-issued photo ID** — driver's license or passport
- **Cashier's check or proof of wire transfer** — for your down payment and closing costs (your closing agent will tell you the exact amount)
- **Any remaining documents** your lender requested

**Important:** Do not bring a personal check. Most closing agents require a cashier's check or wire transfer.

### A Warning About Wire Fraud

If you are wiring money, **verify the wiring instructions directly with your closing agent by phone.** Wire fraud is real. Never send money based on email instructions alone. Always call a number you trust to confirm.

---

## 5 Tips for a Smooth Closing

### 1. Do Not Make Big Financial Changes

Do not open new credit cards, finance furniture, change jobs, or make large deposits or withdrawals. Any of these can affect your loan — even at this late stage.

### 2. Respond Quickly to Requests

If your lender or closing agent asks for a document, get it to them fast. Delays at this point can push back your closing date.

### 3. Ask Questions Early

If anything on your Closing Disclosure does not make sense, ask your mortgage professional before closing day. It is easier to sort out ahead of time.

### 4. Plan Your Schedule

Block out at least two hours. Most closings are shorter, but you do not want to feel rushed on one of the most important days of your life.

### 5. Celebrate

You earned this. When you get those keys, take a moment. You just became a homeowner.

---

## What Happens After Closing

Once everything is signed and the funds are transferred:

- **You get the keys.** The home is officially yours.
- **The deed is recorded** with your local county office.
- **Your first mortgage payment** is typically due 30 to 60 days after closing.

And just because closing is done does not mean we disappear. At START Mortgage, we check in after closing to make sure everything went smoothly. We are here for the long run.
