**Card 1: Pay Every Bill on Time**
**Icon:** Calendar / checkmark
Payment history is the biggest factor in your credit score. Set up autopay or reminders so nothing slips through. Even one missed payment can hurt.

---

**Card 2: Lower Your Credit Card Balances**
**Icon:** Credit card / down arrow
Keep your credit card balances below 30% of your limit. If your limit is $1,000, keep your balance under $300. Lower is better.

---

**Card 3: Don't Open New Accounts**
**Icon:** Shield / stop
Every new credit application creates a hard inquiry on your report. Hold off on new credit cards, car loans, or store accounts while you prepare for a mortgage.

---

**Card 4: Keep Old Accounts Open**
**Icon:** Clock / history
Length of credit history matters. Even if you're not using an old card, keeping it open helps your score.

---

**Card 5: Check for Errors**
**Icon:** Search / magnifying glass
Mistakes on credit reports are more common than you think. Pull your free report at AnnualCreditReport.com. Look for wrong balances, accounts you don't recognize, or late payments that were on time. Dispute anything that's wrong.

---

**Card 6: Work With a Credit Professional**
**Icon:** People / handshake

### SECTION 7: FAQ Accordion (3 Items)
*Inviz Services Section 7*

**Layout:** Elementor Accordion widget. Clean, scannable. Light background.

**Section Tag:**
Common questions

**H2:**
Credit and Mortgage Questions — Answered

**Q1: Can I get a mortgage with bad credit?**
A: It depends on the program and your full financial picture. FHA loans allow credit scores as low as 580 with 3.5% down — and in some cases, scores as low as 500 with a larger down payment. Other programs have different requirements. The best way to find out is to talk about your specific situation. Subject to credit approval.

**Q2: How fast can I improve my credit score?**
A: It depends on what's holding your score down. Some changes — like disputing errors or paying down a credit card — can show results in 30-60 days. Building a strong payment history takes longer, usually 3-6 months of consistent on-time payments.

**Q3: Should I pay off collections before applying for a mortgage?**
A: Not always. In some cases, paying off an old collection can cause your score to dip temporarily. It depends on the type of collection, how old it is, and which loan program you're applying for. Talk to a mortgage professional before taking action.
