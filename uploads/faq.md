#### GROUP 1: Getting Started

**Group Heading (H2):**
Getting Started

---

**Q1: What is a mortgage broker?**
A: A mortgage broker works for you — not for a bank. Instead of offering one set of loan products, we shop programs from multiple lenders to find the right fit for your situation. That means more options and often better terms. START Mortgage is a licensed mortgage broker in Florida (NMLS# 2718409).

[Learn more about how it works ->](/how-it-works)

---

**Q2: How is START Mortgage different from a bank?**
A: When you go to a bank, you see their loan products — and only theirs. When you work with START Mortgage, we compare programs from multiple lenders. We also provide personal guidance from start to finish. You work directly with your loan officer, not a call center. And we offer full service in English and Spanish.

[Meet Jexayra Rivera ->](/about)

---

**Q3: What does a planning session include?**
A: A planning session is a free, no-obligation conversation where we review your financial picture together. You'll learn what you can afford, which loan programs fit your situation, and what your next steps are. If you're ready to move forward, we start the pre-approval process. If you need more time, you leave with a clear plan. Available in English or Spanish.

[Book your free planning session ->](/planning-session)

---

**Q4: How do I get pre-approved?**
A: Pre-approval starts with a completed application and a review of your income, assets, credit, and debts. We match you with the right loan program and issue a pre-approval letter — typically within 24 hours of a completed application. That letter shows sellers and realtors you're a serious, qualified buyer. Subject to credit and underwriting approval.

[Start your pre-approval ->](/pre-approval)

---

**Q5: What credit score do I need?**
A: It depends on the loan program. FHA loans allow credit scores as low as 580 with 3.5% down. Conventional loans typically need 620 or higher. VA loans have no set minimum, but most lenders look for 580-620+. If your score needs work, we'll build you a roadmap to get mortgage-ready.

[Check your credit readiness ->](/credit-readiness)

---

**Q6: Do I need a 20% down payment?**
A: No. That's one of the biggest myths in homebuying. FHA loans require as little as 3.5% down. Some conventional programs start at 3%. VA and USDA loans offer zero-down options for eligible buyers. We'll help you find the program with the lowest out-of-pocket cost for your situation. Programs are subject to eligibility and credit approval.

[See all loan programs ->](/loan-programs)

#### GROUP 2: The Process

**Group Heading (H2):**
The Process

---

**Q7: How long does closing take?**
A: Our average close time is 21 days from the time your loan file is submitted. The full timeline from planning session to keys in hand is typically 45-60 days, depending on your situation. Getting pre-approved early helps speed things up.

---

**Q8: What documents do I need?**
A: You'll typically need:
- Photo ID
- Last 2 years of tax returns
- Last 2 years of W-2s (or 1099s if self-employed)
- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- Last 2 months of bank statements
- Any other documentation related to income, assets, or debts

We'll give you a full checklist during your planning session so nothing is a surprise.

---

**Q9: Can I get a mortgage if I'm self-employed?**
A: Yes. Self-employed buyers qualify for home loans every day. The documentation is a bit different — lenders typically look at 2 years of tax returns and may calculate your income differently. We work with lenders who understand self-employment and know how to present your application in the strongest way possible. Subject to credit and underwriting approval.

---

**Q10: What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?**
A: Pre-qualification is a quick, informal estimate based on what you tell us about your finances. Pre-approval is a deeper review — we verify your income, credit, and assets, and issue a letter that carries real weight with sellers and realtors. At START Mortgage, we focus on pre-approval because it gives you and your realtor confidence that your loan will close.

[What is pre-approval? Full guide ->](/blog/what-is-pre-approval)

---

**Q11: Do you offer Spanish-language service?**
A: Yes. Jexayra Rivera is fully bilingual in English and Spanish. You can complete the entire mortgage process in whichever language you're most comfortable with — from your first planning session through closing day. This isn't just translation. Jexayra was born in Puerto Rico and understands the cultural side of homebuying. *Todo el proceso, en tu idioma.*

---

**Q12: What loan programs do you offer?**
A: We offer a full range of loan programs from multiple lenders, including:
- **FHA Loans** — as low as 3.5% down, credit scores starting at 580
- **Conventional Loans** — as low as 3% down for qualified buyers
- **VA Loans** — zero down for veterans and active military
- **USDA Loans** — zero down in eligible rural and suburban areas
- **Refinance programs** — rate-and-term and cash-out options

We compare options from multiple lenders to find the right fit for your situation. Programs, rates, and terms are subject to change. Not all products are available in all states.

[See all loan programs ->](/loan-programs)
