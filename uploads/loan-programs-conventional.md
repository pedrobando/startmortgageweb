**Body Copy (Text Editor):**
A conventional loan is a mortgage that is not backed by a government agency. It follows guidelines set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac — two organizations that keep the mortgage market stable.

Here is what that means for you:

If you have solid credit and steady income, you can get competitive rates, flexible terms, and more control over your mortgage. Choose between fixed-rate or adjustable-rate. Pick 15, 20, or 30-year terms.

The biggest advantage? No upfront mortgage insurance. And if you pay monthly PMI because you put down less than 20%, it goes away once you reach 80% equity. With FHA, mortgage insurance stays for the life of most loans.

As a mortgage broker, START Mortgage compares conventional options from multiple lenders. One application. Multiple options. The one that fits.

### SECTION 7: FAQ Accordion (3 Items)

**H2:**
Common Questions About Conventional Loans in Kissimmee

**FAQ 1:**
**Q: How much down payment do I need for a conventional loan?**
A: As little as 3% for qualified buyers. Putting down 20% avoids PMI, but it is not required. We will help you weigh the pros and cons based on your savings and goals.

**FAQ 2:**
**Q: When does PMI go away on a conventional loan?**
A: Once your loan balance drops to 80% of the home's value, you can request to remove PMI. This happens as you make payments and your home gains equity. Compare that to FHA loans, where mortgage insurance typically stays for the life of the loan.

**FAQ 3:**
**Q: What credit score do I need for a conventional loan?**
A: Conventional loans generally require higher credit scores than FHA. The exact minimum depends on the lender, down payment, and other factors. During your free planning session, we will review your credit and tell you which programs you qualify for.
