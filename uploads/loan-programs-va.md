A VA loan is a home loan benefit backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. The VA does not lend you the money. It guarantees a portion of the loan — which allows lenders to offer you terms that most other programs cannot match.

Zero down payment. No monthly mortgage insurance. Competitive rates.

VA loans are available to eligible veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard and Reserve members, and certain surviving spouses.

You need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) to use this benefit. We can help you get one.

As a mortgage broker, START Mortgage compares VA loan options from multiple lenders. You get one application, one point of contact, and access to the most competitive terms available.

### SECTION 7: FAQ Accordion (3 Items)

**Layout:** Accordion / toggle widget. Clean, simple. Light background.

**Section Tag:**
Frequently asked questions

**H2:**
Common Questions About VA Loans in Kissimmee

**FAQ 1:**
**Q: Am I eligible for a VA loan?**
A: VA loans are available to veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard and Reserve members, and certain surviving spouses. Eligibility is based on your service history and discharge status. During a free planning session, we will help you check your eligibility and obtain your Certificate of Eligibility (COE).

**FAQ 2:**
**Q: What is the VA funding fee?**
A: The VA funding fee is a one-time fee that supports the VA loan program. The amount depends on your service, down payment, and whether you have used a VA loan before. It can be financed into the loan so you do not pay it out of pocket. Some veterans — including those receiving VA disability compensation — are exempt from this fee.

**FAQ 3:**
**Q: Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once?**
A: Yes. Your VA benefit is reusable. If you have paid off a previous VA loan or sold the home, you may be able to restore your entitlement and use it again on a new purchase. We will help you check your remaining eligibility.
